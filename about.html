MANIFESTO

FOUR THINGS
I BELIEVE.

01

The thinking part is mine.

I’m an attorney. I am not a software engineer. I drive the work. The model drafts, I direct. When you have an attorney in the loop at every stage, it’s a lot easier to see where the problems are and add an extra layer of review, depth, and insight.

02

Bespoke beats one-size-fits-all.

The legal-AI market is built on captive subscriptions to tools that do one thing. This is a bespoke service. You tell me what you need, I scope it, and I make it happen. No annual commit. No stuck-for-a-year regret.

03

Not a threat.

I don’t advertise. I’m not on TV, radio, or buses. Most of my practice is organic to Kentucky and Indiana. Schad AI exists to help other attorneys — especially bigger plaintiff’s firms that have a harder time deploying really useful technology.

04

This is not a secret recipe.

People who work with me will learn a lot about how this stack works. That’s fine. I’m always going to be experimenting with the newest and the best. I use my own cases as the testing ground, and perfect the processes before applying them to other people’s cases.

I PLAN TO STAY ON TOP

As my practice has grown, I’ve shifted toward handling a smaller number of complex cases personally, while being available to help on a much greater number of cases. Helping attorneys in my office, outside my office, and people I do business with.

I like the advocacy part of law, and I’m really not interested in being neutral. Mediation is a different vibe. I want to bring these tools and agentic workflows to other people, so more cases get worked up well.
